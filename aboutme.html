About Me

I'm a cartographer and graduate student at the University of Wisconsin–Madison in the department of Geography. My research intersects cartography and human geography. My specific interests (at the moment) include critical cartography, borders, and mapping experience. Stay tuned to this website for updates!

My work is inspired by a range of past and present cartographers, including: Charles Minard, Kevin Lynch, and Margaret Pearce. My mapping philosophy is to explore classic techniques in a new way, always re-thinking our current cartographies.
